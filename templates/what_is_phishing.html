What is Phishing?

Understanding social engineering and how to stay safe.

How Phishing Works

1. The Initial Contact

Phishing typically begins with a deceptive message sent via email, SMS, or social media. These messages are designed to look like they come from a trusted source, such as your bank, a popular online store, or even a colleague.

2. Creating Urgency

To force you into making a quick decision, the message often creates a sense of urgency or fear. It might claim that your account has been compromised, a payment failed, or that you need to verify your identity immediately to avoid losing access.

3. The Malicious Link

The message contains a link that supposedly takes you to a secure site to resolve the issue. In reality, the link leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one but is controlled by the attacker.

4. Data Theft

Once you enter your username, password, or credit card details on the fake site, the attacker captures this information. They can then use it to access your real accounts, steal your money, or sell your data on the dark web.

Safety Checklist

  • Check sender details: Look for subtle spelling errors in the email address.
  • Verify the URL: Hover over links to see where they actually lead.
  • Don't trust urgency: Be suspicious of any message requiring immediate action with sensitive data.
  • Use 2FA: Enable Two-Factor Authentication on all your important accounts.
